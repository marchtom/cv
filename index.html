A few words about myself
About me
Hello! I'm Tomasz Marchlewski, a dedicated Software Engineer based in Warsaw, Poland. Since March 2021 I am working at IQVIA, where I am serving as a Lead Developer and Software Engineer. Notably, I took the lead in pivotal initiatives for the NFL and NBA COVID-prevention programs. My primary focus has been on designing robust ETL solutions for biomedical data, a critical component in the ongoing fight against the pandemic. Additionally, I've provided valuable consulting services to clients, ensuring that projects align seamlessly with rapidly evolving recommendations.

Embracing DevOps principles, I've gained hands-on experience with various tools and platforms, including Kubernetes, Ansible, Terraform, and Google Cloud Platform. Being part of a dynamic team, I've overseen the build process of system components and executed deployment procedures, contributing to the successful implementation of multifaceted projects. After concluding my role in COVID prevention programs, I seamlessly transitioned to a new team with a focus on creating a platform for clinical tests.

In this new phase, I've continued to thrive as a Lead Developer and Software Engineer, delivering impactful projects, taking ownership of crucial components. Notably, I was involved in the creation of the Medication Lookup, utilizing Azure's Cognitive Search to build a fast and reliable search engine for approved medications. I played a significant role in the Notifications Rule Engine project, orchestrating notification campaigns. The most recent project, Cohort Builder, demanded a flexible solution independent of DevOps or Database Admins support. This was achieved by employing a dedicated Python service responsible for aggregating and storing data in MongoDB, with a GraphQL API exposing MongoDB data to meet our project requirements.

Before joining IQVIA, I worked as a developer at Indoorway. My early tasks involved on-demand spatial data analysis and the creation of tools dedicated to performing specialized work. As the company underwent structural changes, my focus shifted to backend-oriented tasks. I took charge of overseeing the Ruby on Rails GraphQL backend and played a pivotal role in building a robust Python GraphQL infrastructure, starting from the MVP version to a fully functional product.

Beyond the professional landscape, my academic journey includes a PhD in Experimental Nuclear Physics. During my time at the Heavy Ion Laboratory, I engaged in researching the internal structure of the nucleus. Programming and data analysis became integral to my work, as I navigated through well-known tools within the scientific community and developed proficiency in reading and analyzing code.

In my free time, I'm an avid board game collector with around 90 game boxes. I have a particular fondness for euro-economic games but also enjoy the lighter titles for a more relaxed and enjoyable experience. I find the strategic depth of these games to be a delightful contrast to the unpredictability of luck in lighter titles.

Moreover, I've recently discovered a passion for cooking, finding parallels between the culinary world and coding techniques. Crafting self-made meals has become a creative outlet, allowing me to experiment with various flavors and cooking styles. Similar to cooking, I've delved into brewing kombucha as another hands-on hobby. This relatively recent discovery has led me to experiment with different types and flavors of kombucha, adding an exciting dimension to my leisure time.
